What is Medical Negligence?

Medical negligence occurs when healthcare professionals provide substandard care that causes harm to patients. Our Manchester clinical negligence solicitors explain the key elements:

  • Duty of care - Healthcare professional owed you a duty of care
  • Breach of duty - Care fell below acceptable medical standards
  • Causation - The breach directly caused your injury or illness
  • Damage - You suffered harm as a result of the negligent care
  • Reasonable care standard - Treatment didn't meet expected professional standards
  • Expert evidence - Medical experts confirm negligent treatment

Time Limits for Medical Negligence Claims

⏰ Critical Time Limits

  • Standard time limit: 3 years from date of negligence OR date of knowledge
  • Date of knowledge: When you knew negligence caused your injury
  • Fatal cases: 3 years from date of death
  • Child claims: 3 years from 18th birthday (no limit while under 18)
  • Mental capacity issues: No time limit while lacking mental capacity
  • Court discretion: Courts may extend time limits in exceptional cases

⚠️ Critical: Medical negligence cases have complex time limits. Contact us immediately if you suspect medical negligence, regardless of when it occurred.
