What is a Child Arrangement Order?

A Child Arrangement Order is a court order that determines arrangements for children following family breakdown. Our Manchester child custody solicitors handle all aspects:

  • Lives with arrangements - Which parent children live with
  • Spends time with - Contact arrangements with other parent
  • Otherwise has contact - Phone calls, video calls, letters
  • Specific conditions - Supervised contact, handover arrangements
  • Holiday arrangements - Christmas, school holidays, birthdays
  • International travel - Taking children abroad

Types of Child Arrangement Applications

Our Manchester child arrangement specialists handle various application types:

🏠 Lives With Orders

  • Primary residence - Where children live most of the time
  • Shared residence - Children spend equal time with both parents
  • Change of residence - Moving children between parents
  • Grandparent residence - Children living with extended family
  • Emergency residence - Urgent protection applications

⏰ Spends Time With Orders

  • Regular contact - Weekly or fortnightly visits
  • Holiday contact - Extended stays during school breaks
  • Overnight stays - Weekend contact arrangements
  • Supervised contact - Contact with professional supervision
  • Indirect contact - Phone, video, or written communication

📞 Otherwise Has Contact Orders

  • Phone contact - Regular telephone conversations
  • Video calls - Skype, FaceTime, WhatsApp contact
  • Written contact - Letters, cards, emails
  • Social media contact - Age-appropriate online communication
  • School contact - Attending school events and meetings

Child Welfare - Primary Consideration

⚖️ Children's Best Interests

Our Manchester child custody lawyers ensure applications focus on:

  • Child's wishes and feelings - Age-appropriate consideration
  • Physical and emotional needs - Health, education, development
  • Likely effect of change - Impact on child's circumstances
  • Age, sex, background - Individual child characteristics
  • Risk of harm - Physical, emotional, or psychological harm
